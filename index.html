Meet Eleanor

In 1915, a remarkable woman signed a petition that would echo through history. Her name appears in the International Women's Peace Petition, alongside thousands of others who dared to imagine a world without war.

But Eleanor's story doesn't end there. Her life weaves through our archives like a golden thread, connecting moments of courage, creativity, and conviction.

The Peace Petition

Here, in faded ink on weathered paper, we find Eleanor's signature among the brave women who stood against the Great War. Each name represents a story, a hope, a declaration of peace.

1915: Over 1,000 women from across Britain signed this petition, demanding their voices be heard in matters of war and peace.

A Life Revealed

Following Eleanor's trail through our collections reveals a woman of many talents. Here she appears in a society portrait from 1912 - young, determined, already showing signs of the activist she would become.

Notice the subtle details: her pose, her expression, the way she holds herself. This is someone ready to change the world.

Letters of Purpose

In her correspondence from 1920, we see Eleanor's continued commitment to social justice. Her handwriting, forceful and clear, speaks of certainty and purpose.

She writes: "The work of peace is not finished with the war's end, but has only just begun."
